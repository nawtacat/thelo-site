SAT Math Production Blueprint

Our methodology for building a world-class course creation team.

Step 1: Define the Vision

Before recruiting, we establish a rock-solid foundation by defining:

  • The Product: A high-quality, voice-driven, handwriting-first SAT Math course.
  • The Timeline: Pilot in 2 months, full rollout in 6.
  • The Workflow: Script → VO → Drawing → Integration → QA → Launch.

Step 2: Define Roles

We break the work into core roles for ownership and expertise.

Curriculum Lead (2): Define lesson objectives, scope, flow.
Scriptwriters (2–3): Draft narration, MCQs, hints using AI.
Voice Talent (4): Provide diverse accents and clean delivery.
Drawing Artist (1): Match audio with handwriting, build JSONs.
QA / Tester (1): Spot issues in grading, flow, and timing.
Project Manager (1): Keep timelines, assets, and people on track.

Step 3: Source Candidates

  • Personal Networks: Tap into trusted collaborators from RBC, UWC, and mentor circles.
  • Targeted Outreach: Post clear role descriptions in relevant university and tech groups.
  • Screen for Fit: Use short, paid test tasks (e.g., sample script, voice demo) to evaluate responsiveness, detail, and skill.

Step 4: Onboard the Crew

  • Create a Workspace: Centralize communication on Discord or Slack.
  • Build a Knowledge Base: Use Google Drive or Notion for assets, progress trackers, and naming conventions.
  • Run a Kickoff Call: Introduce the mission, walk through the production pipeline, and set clear expectations.

Step 5: Pilot the Process

We start with 1–2 lessons and run them through the full production cycle: Script → Voice → Drawing → Integration → QA. This allows the team to experience the flow end-to-end, spotting inefficiencies and bottlenecks before scaling up.

Step 6: Plan Weekly Rhythm

We set a sustainable weekly cadence for production:

  • Monday: Assign new lessons.
  • Midweek: Review script and voice drafts.
  • Friday: Integrate and perform QA.

Step 7: Maintain Culture

We maintain momentum by celebrating wins, encouraging open feedback loops, being transparent about timelines, and keeping the core mission—making math accessible and human—at the forefront of our work.

Step 8: Scale Gradually

Once the production system is proven and efficient, we can expand our impact by adding more scriptwriters, localizing content to other languages, launching with schools, and expanding into new subjects.
