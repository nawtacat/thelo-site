Step 1: Define the Vision and Workflow

Before recruiting, we establish a rock-solid foundation. This sets the standard for quality and informs every hiring decision.

  • Product Vision: A high-quality, voice-driven, handwriting-first SAT Math course.
  • Key Timeline: Pilot release in 2 months; full course rollout in 6 months.
  • Production Pipeline: A clear, linear process: Script → Voice-Over → Drawing → Integration → Quality Assurance → Launch.

Step 2: Break the Work into Roles

Curriculum Lead (2 Positions)

Core Focus: Define lesson objectives, curriculum scope, and the logical flow of topics.

Scriptwriter (2-3 Positions)

Core Focus: Draft engaging narration, multiple-choice questions, and helpful hints, leveraging AI for efficiency.

Voice Talent (4 Positions)

Core Focus: Provide clean, professional audio delivery with a diverse range of clear accents.

Drawing Artist & Integrator (1 Position)

Core Focus: Create the visual handwriting demonstrations synchronized to audio and assemble the final lesson JSON files.

QA & Testing Specialist (1 Position)

Core Focus: Meticulously check for issues in answer grading, lesson flow, audio/visual timing, and overall user experience.

Project Manager (1 Position)

Core Focus: Own the production schedule, manage assets, and ensure seamless communication to keep the team on track.

Step 3: Source Candidates Strategically

We source talent through trusted channels, prioritizing proven skill and clear communication.

  • Personal Networks: We tap into our existing networks of trusted, proven collaborators from UWC, RBC, and mentor circles.
  • Targeted Outreach: We post clear, concise role descriptions to relevant professional and academic groups, offering fair compensation and valuable portfolio work.
  • Screen for Fit: We use short, paid test tasks to assess not just skill, but also responsiveness, attention to detail, and communication clarity.

Step 4: Onboard the Crew

A structured onboarding process ensures every team member is aligned and equipped for success from day one.

  • Centralize a Workspace: All communication is centralized in a shared hub like Slack or Discord.
  • Create a Knowledge Base: All assets, progress trackers, and documentation (like naming conventions) live in a central repository like Notion or Google Drive.
  • Run a Kickoff Call: We host a mandatory kickoff call to introduce the mission and team, walk through the pipeline, and set clear expectations for communication and deadlines.

Step 5: Pilot a Mini Production Cycle

Before committing to full-scale production, we run 1-2 lessons through the entire workflow. This end-to-end test allows the team to experience the process, identify any inefficiencies or confusion, and refine our approach before scaling.

Step 6: Plan a Weekly Rhythm

We establish a sustainable weekly production cadence to ensure consistent progress. A simple Kanban board on Notion or Trello is used for lesson tracking.

  • Monday: New lesson assignments are distributed.
  • Midweek: Script and voice-over drafts are reviewed.
  • Friday: Final assets are integrated and sent to QA.

Step 7: Maintain Culture + Momentum

Technology and process are only half the equation. We actively foster a positive and focused culture by celebrating wins, encouraging transparent feedback, and consistently reinforcing our shared mission to make math education more accessible and human.

Step 8: Scale Gradually

Once our production system is proven to be efficient and reliable, we can begin to scale our impact. This includes adding more content creators, localizing the course for other languages, forming partnerships with schools, and expanding the curriculum into new subjects like Geometry and Precalculus.
